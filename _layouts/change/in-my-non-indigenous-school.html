  • School Board

    (Also called: School district, School division, School council, District education council, Divisional education council)

    School boards make decisions for the group of schools within its area. (For example: they implement the curriculum, make hiring and firing decisions, and decide when and how money should be spent on schools.)

    • Provincial/Territorial Government

    • Superintendent

      The head of a school board is called the superintendent, and the members are called trustees or members.

In some remote areas, there are no school boards, and schools are operated directly by the government.

Private schools may or may not have school boards. If you go to a private school, you might want to look on your schoolâ€™s website, or ask someone at your school, whether there is a school board.
